COVID-19 in Mexico

How does it look?

An overview of cases in Mexican states and municipalities

By Yuriria Avila

On February 28, Mexico had its first registered case of Coronavirus. The 35-year-man had previously traveled from Italy. By then Italy had 650 confirmed cases of Coronavirus.

On March 23 Mexico's Secretary of Health registered a positive case of COVID-19 from someone who hadn't traveled from Europe or had been in contact with someone who had. The virus was trasmited locally. Therefore, on March 24, Mexico's President, Andrés Manuel López Obrador, announced the beginning of Phase 2.

By then, Mexico had 930 confirmed cases of Coronavirus.

On April 21, Health Undersecretary, Hugo Lopez Gatell, declared the beginning of Phase 3. This means that the highest peak of contagiousness is expected in the following days. According to his estimation, this day will be May 8.

On April 21, Mexico had 14 thousand 658 confirmed cases.

How do cases look on a map?

This map shows the total number of positive cases of COVID 19 since January. Even though the first COVID-19 case found was on February 28, a person who later tested positive on COVID-19 was hospitalized on January 8th and was included on the database.

As it is visible, most cases are concentrated in the center of the country. Mexico City and the State of Mexico are the states with the highest number of COVID-19 cases.

Mexico City has 6,999 cases while the State of Mexico has 4,353.

According to the municipality- level data, 6 out of 10 municipalities with the highest positive cases are located in Mexico City or the State of Mexico.

The scenario is similar for cases per one hundred people. 3 out of 10 municipalities with the highest number of cases per 100 people are in Mexico City or the State of Mexico.

How have cases changed weekly?

In the last five weeks, between April 12 and May 5, cases have spread all over the country. Even though most cases have concentrated in states such as Mexico City, State of Mexico, northern states such as Baja California and Sinaloa and southern states like QuintanaRoo, it is visible how new cases have appeared in municipalities around the country.

How many cases are active?

Active cases mean the number of people who have tested positive and have had symptoms for the past 14 days. For this map, cases are between April 22 and May 5. During this period there were 6 thousand 708 cases all over the country.

Cases are active just in 9 states and in 13 municipalities according to data. Tamaulipas, Ciudad de Mexico, and Chiapas are the states with the highest number of active cases.

How have active cases changed weekly?

Similar to the first animation map, it is visible that most cases are concentrated in Mexico City. But for other states and municipalities, cases have been moving around the country. This means, that besides Mexico City, municipalities that are infecting are recovering.

How many people have died so far?

On March 18, the first patient who had tested positive for COVID-19 passed away. Since then, 2 thousand 507 people have died all over the country. Three out of ten municipalities with the highest death rate are located in Mexico City.

This information is sensitive and some people might find it triggering or upsetting. Therefore, I decided not to create an animation on how death has increased for the past five weeks.
